    • Packing Process

  • Packing Overview:

    Operate a station (1-12) using a computer, scanner, and poly bags / boxes to fulfill orders Properly pack & handle “.com” / Amazon and wholesale orders Separate orders into appropriate bin .com & wholesale orders go onto truck Amazon & FedEx 2Day orders go in FedEx bin USPS orders go into USPS carrier bag Keep stations properly stocked with needed daily materials (poly bags, tape, etc.) and clean

    Skills to Know:

    How to pack an order in under 1 minute How to navigate through ShipStation (Orders , Products , Scan) How to properly build & pack boxes No boxes over 50lbs Write # on box for shipping labels Thin cardboard underneath & above clothes Packing Slip INSIDE the box Only “Marshmallow” clothing into 24x14x20 sized box Be able to tell the difference between a new item and a refurbished item Wholesale orders MUST have new items (sticker on poly bag in bottom-right corner) .com / Amazon orders can be sent refurbished items There should never be two closed & unlabeled bags at a station; work with ONE order at a time. How to pack a “.com” / Amazon order: Make sure that the program “ShipStation Connect” is open & logged in. Open “shipstation.com” & navigate to “Scan” screen Grab ONE packing slip out of black bin & all items underneath until you seen another packing slip Use the scanner on the barcode at the bottom of the packing slip Once the order is up on the screen, scan all of the items until it shows a green verification screen. All correctly scanned items should make a ping noise as you scan Select bag size (med/large poly) or box size (24x14x6, 17x14x10, etc.) Amazon orders will go into FedEx poly bag when able (FedEx One Rate Pak) Click or scan “Print” Fold the packing slip & place into bag or box. Pull adhesive strip on poly bags or use tape to seal box. Make sure poly bag is completely sealed from left to right. This is to ensure packages do not get stuck to each other. Shipping label will come from thermal printer; center it neatly on poly / put in corner of box and firmly apply label

    How to pack a “Wholesale” order:

    Repeat steps 1-4 above i> Separate items by SKU, color and size i> Scan items, starting at the largest size of a SKU, and place items into a box one at a time. As you are scanning a SKU, put all colors together (ie, 8900-8-Black-2X-3X, 8900-8-Black-XL, 8900-8-Coco-2X-3X, 8900-8-Coco-XL, etc.). Once everything is sized, scanned, and properly packed into boxes, use a scale to weigh each box Input the weights and dimensions of each box into shipstation Click “Print” Wholesale orders should ship FedEx Ground unless otherwise specified Put the numbered label on the corresponding box All boxes are ready to be loaded onto the truck

    • Returns Process

    Please watch this video to see what the returns process looks like from start to finish

    Physically Receiving Packages

    Returns come in daily from USPS, FedEx and UPS. Return packages are placed inside the green gaylord located at the third dock door. You can pick these packages up using a cart or buggy and should bring them to the returns area located in the back of the warehouse. If the gaylord is completely full it will need to be moved with a pallet jack and replaced with an empty one. During peak times these packages will need to be seperated by date of arrival. Please label each batch of returns according to the arrival date.

    Inspection/Quality Control

    Work on one package at a time, carefully opening packages so you don't accidentally damage items. It's best to first make a small opening and then carefully make it wider. Once the package is open lay out items for inspection making sure to keep bottoms and tops together from each set. When inspecting different sets of pajamas, it's best to work on one pair at a time to avoid accidentally mixing sizes. Carefully inspect each item for any defects. Lint rollers should be used to remove lint. Sometimes small blemishes can be removed by wiping it with a paper towel. If an item is too soiled with pet hair it will need to be damaged out. Small loose thread can be carefully trimmed. Smell items for perfume or bad odors, to ensure they have not been washed or used. We do our best to refurbish items and save them from being damaged out but keep in mind that they need to be in sellable condition. Our goal is for customers to always have a positive experience when they receive or product.

    Documentation

    We typically receive a packing slip inside return packages. If you can't find one trim the label off the package and use that instead. Make sure the date of arrival, item number, color, size, last four numbers of UPC, item quantity, order number and condition of the items is documented. If any info is missing, please document as much information on the return as possible. If order number is missing, use the name of the sender and tracking number. Please keep all documentation on returns in an orderly fashion. These slips of papers should seperated by date and be handed to Rosa or Karla for the next step in the receiving process.

    Repackaging

    After items are inspected and cleaned they should be folded and bagged, then put on a cart or gaylord to later be put back on shelves. Please make sure you are neatly folding items to look as good as new. If you're unsure how something should be folded ask Karla or Rosa and they will help you.

    Retags

    Items that are in good condition but missing a tag will be documented as retagged. These items should be folded and bagged as normal but will be put in boxes in the retag area. If the tag is just detached we have tag guns to reattach it and can show you how to use it.

    Damages

    Damaged items will also need to be documented. Explain the damage in writing as best as possible and place in a bag along with documentation. Damaged returns go in the damaged section inside small black bins to later be photographed. After they are photographed they are placed inside a box for donation.

    Clean Up

    Clean up as you go to ensure we are keeping a clutter free and safe working environment. Boxes should be cut with box cutters and disposed of in red bins. Once the red bins are full empty contents in dumpster. Throw away plastic trash into black garbage bins. Once the trash is full please take out bag and replace it.

    Inventory Process

    After returns are inspected they are entered into a daily receiving worksheet and received into Netsuite.

    Put Away

    The final step in the returns process is placing items back on the shelves. In order to do so you will be given a put away sheet which will show you the location of each item inside the warehouse. Carefully go through the list and make sure you are neatly placing items back in the correct area. A clipboard and highlighter will be provided. If there are any items missing from the put away sheet, let Karla or Rosa know and they will help you.
